EXPLORE PAKISTAN

Pakistan is a country located in South Asia. Pakistan is a nation not just defined by its natural beauty and architectural splendours but by its friendly inhabitants, varied wildlife and rich culinary traditions. The South Asian nation topped several international travel lists such as Forbes' "The 10 coolest places to go in 2019" and Conde Nast's "best holiday destination in 2020," according to a media report.

Hunza Valley

Hunza Valley is in the Gilgit-Baltistan province in Pakistan. Hunza Valley is located at a height of 2438 meters above sea level, where the weather can often drop below zero degrees. Karimabad, the state capital of Hunza, offers a panoramic view of the Rakaposhi peaks. There is nothing better than staying in the Hurza Valley for days, or even weeks or months, along the legendary Karakoram Highway. Certainly, the valley deserves its nickname of paradise. At the top of the Valley, you are greeted with The Baltit Fort which was built in 800BC. The valley is also home to The Eagle's Nest which is a popular viewpoint of with a breathtaking view of the valley especially during sunrise and sunsets. The valley also consists of Ultar base camp which is heaven for hiking/trekking lovers with a view of glaciers along the way.

Faisal Mosque

Faisal Mosque is one of Pakistan's largest mosques, located in the nation's capital, Islamabad. With a total area of 54,000 square feet, it is the sixth largest mosque on the planet. A mixture of ultramodern and traditional architectural styles contributes to its status as one of Asia's largest. The mosque is situated on a hilltop with a beautiful view of the Margalla Hills in the background. Nearly 300,000 worshippers can be accommodated in the mosque and its courtyard.

Hingol National Park

A total of 1,650 sq kilometers of Hingol National Park can be found along the Makran Coastal Highway. The park is also classified as a semi-coastal desert because of its large area of drift sand. Among Pakistan's key natural reserves, the park is regarded as an important site. Because of natural factors, there are many different landscapes in this park. Princess Of Hope is an eroded rock formation resembling a princess formed by weathering and erosion. Another rock formation shaped by chemical and physical weathering is a sphinx known as The Lion of Balochistan. This structure is similar to that in Egypt, but formed by nature. It also contains lakes, ponds and streams, which are surrounded by vegetation in this deserted park. Kund Malir Beach, with its crystal clear water, is another addition to the park. The park also has natural mud volcanoes. This geographical feature is also a sacred site for Hindus. In a cave, the minority hindu community also has a temple where they perform a four-day pilgrimage.

Transport


Air: Domestic airlines work within the country by Pakistan International Airlines from city to city. Airlines including Shaheen Air, AirBlue offer daily day and night flights to cities like Peshawar, Faisalabad, Lahore, Islamabad, Quetta, Multan, Karachi and Skardu.

Rail:Pakistan Railways operates throughout the country from one major city to another from day and night. These trains are divided in different types of classes: air-con sleeper, economy class, first class etc. Children under three travel for free and children between 3-11 are charged half the fare.

Coach: The Daewoo Express runs through major cities in Pakistan, providing a comfortable and luxurious travelling experience. Each coach comes with air-con, screens for each seat and snacks for each passenger hosted by a trained hostess. There are also porters on sight offering free service to assist in taking care of your luggage.

Weather

Throughout the year, Pakistan experiences three different types of seasons. Summer (April-July) has extreme temperatures predominantly in the southern part of the country, while monsoon (July-September) is when precipitation rates on hills are highest. Winter (March-November) is warm, with sea breezes along the southern coast and snow on the peaks in northern Pakistan.
The best time to visit southern Pakistan is between November and March when the days are cooler and clear with no humidity. The best time to visit northern Pakistan is from April to October during this time there is less snow and ideal for trekking in northern mountainous areas. During winter, the northern part of the country stays covered in a blanket of snow.

Languages

The two official languages of the nation are Urdu and English. However, due to the diverse cultures within its land, there are between 70-80 other languages spoken across the five provinces of Pakistan. These include Punjabi, Sindhi, Pashto, Sariki and Balti.
