Victoria Falls

Victoria Falls is recgonised as one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World with over 5 million cubic metres of water dropping over every minute in peak season earning the landmark the title of the World's Largest Waterfall. Located in the heart of Southern Africa on the border between Zimbabwe and Zambia the landmark attracts millions of tourists from around the globe to observe its natural beauty.The spray from the falls usually rises to a height of over 400 metres but on some occasions the spray rises twice as high and can be seen from 50km away. On a full moon a moonbow can be sighted in the spray.

Short facts about Vicotria Falls
Has seven gorges
Named after Queen Victoria
Part of the Zambezi River

The park was first established in the 1950's.The park is largely unexplored and has little-known about it yet that parts that are known holds a fantastic diversity of wildlife with the park also being known as a birders paradise with roughly the more or the same amount of species within the park than the whole of Europe with Black cheecked lovebirds,Chaplin's Barbet and African Fin-Foot being amoung the plethora of birds living within the park. Lake Tanganyika

Despite being a landlocked country Zambia holds 7% of the longest lake in the world within the countrys' borders.First revealed to the European world by the English explorers John Speke and Richard Burton in a journey that is documented in the movie 'Mountains of the Moon'. It is the longest fresh water lake in the world and is 1433 metres(4,700feet) deep making it the second deepest after lake Baikal located in Russia. The clear water the lake holds is well known for its aquarium fish exports and its world class angling.
