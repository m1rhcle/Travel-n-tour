Fortress São Miguel

Saint Michael Fortress or Fortaleza de São Miguel was a fortress built in the capital of Angola,Luanda by the Portuguese who were the then rulers of the country the fort was built in 1576. Within the the forts thick walls there are elaborate ceramic tiles which tell the story of Angola under Portuguese rule with the courtyard having large statues of the first Portuguese king, Vasco da Gama and the first European to reach Angol Diogo Cão among other notable figures.The fortress was known as the headquarters of the Commander in Chief of the Portuguese Armed Forces in Angola but today it now holds the Museum of the Armed Forces.

Short facts about Fortress São Miguel
Fort was a self-contained town protected by thick walls encrusted with cannons
In 1641, occupied by netherlanders, the Fortress is object of the first real attack; in 1648 the battle is finised with the surrender and expelling of the netherlander troops by the portugueses.
The Fortress is preserved and it had not any desintegration.

Palácio de Ferro

Also known as the Iron Palace is a historical building in the city of Luanda.It is believed to be designed and built by someone associated with the bulder of the Eiffel Tower and the Statue of Liberty Gustave Eiffel. The structure has a mysterious history because there is no record of its existence but,it is believed to have been pre-built in France during the 1890s and was heading for Madagascar by boat but was instead drifted by the harsh Benguela currents and ended up in Angola's Skeleton Coast where it was later claimed by the Portuguese,and was made into an art center. The palace was neglected after Angola gained independence and then was heavily damaged during the countrys' civil war. .

Memorial Dr. António Agostinho Neto(MAAN)

This monument which is also known as the Foguetão was built in honor of António Agostinho Neto who was an Angolan doctor,writer and politician he was seen as the country's main figure in the 20th century.After Agostinho Neto's death on the 10th of September 1979,the Angolan government commissioned the former USSR to design the Mausoleum to house the President's remains.The first brick was layed on September 17 1982 and in 1998 the project was reformulated and work restarted in 2005 and was finally completed in January of 2011.With an area of approximately 18 hectares, the memorial has a central block which contains the remains of Agostinho Neto.The building contains a museum,exhibiion gallery,multipurpose rooms,shops and much more.
