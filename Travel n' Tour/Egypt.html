Giza Necropolis

The Giza pyramid complex,also called the Giza necropolis, is the site on the Giza Plateau in the Greater Cairo area.This location includes the Great Pyramid of Giza,the Pyramid of Menkaure and the associated pyramid complexes and the Great Sphinx of Giza.Which were all built during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom of Ancient Egypt which was between 2600 and 2500 BC. The Pyramid of Khafre and The Great Pyramid of Giza are the largest pyramids built in ancient Egypt and symbolises Ancient Egypt in western imagination. The pyramids are the oldest and only Ancient Wonder that still exits today

Short facts about Giza Necropolis
Khufu's pyramid, known as the great pyramid of Giza, is the oldest and largest, rising at 481 feet (146 meters) .
The Sphinx is a mythical creature found in Greek, Egyptian and even South Asian ancient architecture.
The Giza pyramids have elaborate tunnel systems inside containing gold and other objects Egyptians thought would be useful in the afterlife.

Karnak

The Karnak Temple Complex or Karnak is made up of decayed temples,chapels,pylons and multiple other buildings near Luxor,Egypt. Construction began during the reign of Senusret I in the Middle Kingdom and continued into the Ptolemaic Kingdom,although most of the buildings that are still standing are from the New Kingdom. The area surrounding Karnak was the Ancient Egyptian Ipet-isut and the primary place of worship of the 18th Dynastic Theban Triad. In 1979 it was added onto the UNESCO World Heritage List along with the rest of the city.

Short facts about Karnak
Karnak considered to be a very sacred place in Ancient Egypt
Some of the buildings were turned into Christian churches in the 4th century.
Construction on the site is believed to have happened for nearly 2,000 years (Between 2000 and 1700 B.C. and lasted until between 305 and 30 B.C. .

Valley of the Kings

The Valley of Kings also known as The Valley of the Gates of the Kings,is a valley in Egypt located on the west bank of the Nile,opposite Thebas,within the heart of the Theban Necropolis. For almost 500 years during the 16th to 11th century BC,tombs were cut out of the rock and excavated for pharaohs and powerful nobles of the time. It consists of two valleys :The East Valley and The West Valley.The royal tombs are decorated with scenes and images from Egyptian mythology and gives insight into the beliefs,funeral and burial practices of the time. With the 2005 discovery of a new chamber added by the further discovery of two further tomb entrances, the valley continues to stimulate interest and research worldwide.
